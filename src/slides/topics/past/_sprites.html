CSS Sprites were born from HTTP/1.1 limitations - fewer requests meant better performance. But the maintenance cost was HUGE.

this idea leaded to lots of problems

  • ⚠️ Add one button → rebuild entire sprite
  • ⚠️ Manual pixel calculations
  • ⚠️ Hover/Active states = 2/3x the complexity
  • ⚠️ Different sizes? More sprites!
  • ⚠️ Documentation needed to track positions
With HTTP/2 multiplexing, the "fewer requests" argument died. SVG gave us scalable, accessible icons. Sprites became a relic of the past.
